Key Messaging Points for Rottman

1Their Own Records Drive the Number
BCG analyzed 35,272 workweeks and 170,350 shifts from VERIDIAM 000486-488. The face of the time records yields a 20.4% meal violation rate, 99.6% no-2nd-meal on 10–12 hr shifts, and 82.3% missed 2nd meals on shifts over 12 hours. Premium payments offset 0.5% of meal violations. This is not advocacy — it's their own time and pay data.
2Rest Break Policy Is Defective on Its Face
Class members are not permitted to leave the premises for rest breaks — a direct contradiction of Augustus v. ABM Security Servs. (2016) 2 Cal.5th 257. That alone supports a 100% violation rate and the $5.37M rest premium claim. There is no operational data that gets defendant out of Augustus — it's a policy-level violation.
3"Fake" Meal Periods Are the Real Story
Furnace operators and CNC operators can't leave running machines or parts unattended — yet they were directed to clock out, set 30-minute timers, and keep working. That records as a compliant meal break and entirely defeats the headline 20.4% rate. Class member testimony across three departments (furnace, machine, CNC) is consistent and corroborating.
4Plaintiff Douglas Is Outside the Arbitration Cohort
Douglas's employment ended 1/7/25 — a month before the 2/2025 rollout. He's not a signatory and never will be. PAGA representative claims survive entirely under Adolph v. Uber. Even the signatory cohort faces strong OTO/Davis v. Kozak procedural unconscionability — mandatory HR meeting, on-the-spot signature, no take-home, presenter angry at questions. The "Updated September 2024" date is a tell that they've been deploying without a prior version. The signing-date roster they promised is overdue since 4/10/26.
5Every Premium Paid Is an Underpaid Premium (Ferra)
The 162 paid premiums all exclude shift-differential and bonus codes that Ferra v. Loews (2021) 11 Cal.5th 858 requires in the regular rate. That converts every "compliant" payment into an automatic §226 wage-statement violation and §203 derivative — without further fact development. This is a structural problem in their payroll, not an outlier.
6PAGA Stands Independently — $4.92M
324 aggrieved employees. 8,927 PAGA pay periods. 99.9% rest break violation rate across the PAGA period; 46.2% net meal violations. Not arbitrable post-Adolph. PAGA penalties for waiting time, wage statements, reimbursements, overtime, and meal premiums each stack independently. This exposure exists even if every Class certification battle goes the wrong way.
