Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths (push hard)

  • Dual timekeeping system — Def's own Handbook §11 says door-punch is the official time record, yet payroll runs off flat "8 hour" synthetic sheets. This is the headline. Every time they push back, come back to: "your own handbook."
  • 1.9% premium payment rate against a 53.4% meal violation rate. Impossible to explain away.
  • Union vs. non-union asymmetry — Towson's union field timecards track Break 1/Lunch/Break 2. Office non-union sheets track nothing. Proves capacity, proves choice, sets up Donohue adverse inference on rest breaks.
  • No AWS election on file with DIR — pattern of 4/10 and 5/9 in the door data means every hour over 8/day is facially unpaid OT.
  • Non-neutral rounding, two layers — 59.8% of shifts underpaid, avg 2.9 min/shift. Camp / Woodworth directly on point.
  • Witness testimony corroborates — Bradley ("manager wrote 'eight hours' per day regardless"; "can you please clock out at 5PM?") and Green ("encouraged to quietly remove that time"). Two witnesses in two departments.
  • FCRA/ICRAA standalone disclosure — HireRight bundled form is a free-standing $250K–$350K claim Def hasn't accounted for.
  • 239 non-arb class members (15,223 ww) are firm regardless of arb ruling — that's a floor Def cannot argue away.

Weaknesses (know your blind spots)

  • 174 arb agreements sprung 4/2/2026 — one week before mediation. Def will lead with this. Our response: procedural unconscionability, late disclosure, pre-certification pick-off concerns, Cook v. USC scrutiny — and in any event, 239 members remain.
  • Meal period method relies on door-punch gaps as proxy — Def will argue these gaps include rest breaks, personal errands, etc. Brief concedes the methodology "overstates compliant meal breaks" — Def will flip that to attack.
  • OTC claim based on 1 hr/wk assumption — conservative but not data-driven. Def will call it speculative.
  • Single undated Handbook (v5.1) — we can't pin down which version governed which part of the class period.
  • Rest break claim is 100% assumption — Donohue-inference based, not data-based. Strong legal posture but soft on numbers.
  • Co-counsel PAGA uncertainty — Otkupman losing contact with Towson could complicate apportionment and expose us to a "split the difference" mediator pressure to absorb PAGA without credit.
  • No insurance carrier involvement noted — Def paying out of pocket, which caps top-end willingness.

Messaging Points (Repeat All Day)

  • "This is a data case, not a lawyer argument." Every major number comes from Control Air's own fob data and payroll records. We didn't make up the 53.4% meal violation rate — BCG pulled it from their production.
  • "Their handbook says the door-punch is the official time record. They just chose not to use it." — Handbook p.11. This is the line for Gig.
  • "They track break times for union field workers but leave non-union office timesheets blank." — The capacity-vs-choice asymmetry is the moral framing for the adverse inference.
  • "1.9% premium payment on a 53.4% violation rate." — Numeric soundbite. Use it every move.
  • On arbitration: "239 members with 15,223 workweeks have no arb agreement at all. Even if every one of the 174 sprung agreements is enforceable — and we'll show they're not — the case doesn't go away."
  • On the late-disclosed arb: "You handed us 174 arbitration agreements one week before a scheduled mediation. That tells us exactly how these were rolled out and what the circumstances of signature were. We're not discounting this case for agreements we haven't even seen."
  • On FCRA/ICRAA: "That's a separate $250K–$350K claim from a separate statute. It's not in the $26M. Don't ask us to fold it in for free."
