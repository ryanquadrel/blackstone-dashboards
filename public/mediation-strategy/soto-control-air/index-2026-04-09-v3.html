Key Messaging Points for Kyriacou

1This Is a Data Case, Not a Lawyer Argument
Every major number comes from Control Air's own door-punch and payroll records. BCG pulled the 53.4% meal violation rate straight out of Defendant's production. We didn't make up the 59.8% rounding underpayment figure — their system did.
2Their Handbook Says It — They Just Ignored It
Handbook p.11: door-punch IS the official time record. Yet payroll runs off flat "8 hour" synthetic weekly time sheets with no start times, no end times, no meal periods, no rest breaks. That's not a dispute — that's an admission.
3Capacity vs. Choice — The Union Asymmetry
Towson's union field timecards track Break 1 Start/End, Lunch Start/End, Break 2 Start/End. Non-union office timesheets track nothing. Control Air had the infrastructure — they chose not to deploy it for the office workers. That's the Donohue adverse inference in one sentence.
41.9% Premium Payment on 53.4% Violation Rate
